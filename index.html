Timeline

  • 1964

    Born in Beirut, Lebanon on September 2, 1964, the son of Patricia (née Taylor), an English costume designer and performer from Essex, and Samuel Nowlin Reeves, Jr., an American man from Hawaii.


  • 1971

    When was 15, worked as a production assistant on Aaron's films.


  • 1980

    Following this film's critical success, I spent the late 1980s appearing in a number of movies aimed at teenage audiences, including Permanent Record, and the unexpectedly successful 1989 comedy, Bill & Ted's Excellent Adventure, along with its 1991 sequel, Bill & Ted's Bogus Journey.


  • 1980

    I began my acting career at the age of nine, appearing in a theatre production of Damn Yankees. At 15, I played Mercutio in a stage production of Romeo and Juliet at the Leah Posluns Theatre. I dropped out of high school when I was 17. Then obtained a green card through my American stepfather and moved to Los Angeles three years later. I lived with my ex-stepfather, Paul Aaron, who was a stage and television director. I made my screen acting debut in an episode of Hangin' In. In the early 1980s, I appeared in commercials (including one for Coca-Cola), short films including the NFB drama One Step Away and stage work such as Brad Fraser's cult hit Wolfboy in Toronto.


  • 1986

    My first studio movie appearance was Youngblood (1986) in which I played a Québécois goalie.


  • 1986

    After a few minor roles, Reeves received a sizeable role in the 1986 drama film River's Edge, which depicted how a murder affected a group of teens.


  • 1990

    During the early 1990s, I started to break out of my teen-film period.


  • 1991

    I was involved in various lower-budget independent films, including the well-received 1991 film, My Own Private Idaho with River Phoenix.


  • 1992

    Appeared in high-budget action films like Point Break, for which I won MTV's "Most Desirable Male" award in 1992.


  • 1994

    In 1994 career reached a new high as a result of my starring role in the action film Speed. My casting in the film was controversial, since, except for Point Break, I was primarily known for comedies and indie dramas. I had never been the sole headliner on a film. The summer action film had a fairly large budget and was helmed by novice cinematographer-turned-director Jan de Bont. My career choices after Speed were eclectic: despite successes, I continued to accept supporting roles and appear in experimental films. I scored a hit with a romantic lead role in A Walk in the Clouds.


  • 1995

    Made news by refusing to take part in Speed 2: Cruise Control – despite the offered $11 million paycheck, which would have been my largest to date – in favour of touring with my band and playing the title role in a 1995 Manitoba Theatre Centre production of Hamlet in Winnipeg, Manitoba. However, I choosing after A Walk in the Clouds failed with critics and audiences. Big-budget films such as the sci-fi action film Johnny Mnemonic and the action-thriller Chain Reaction were critically panned and failed at the box office, while indie films like Feeling Minnesota were also critical failures. I finally started to climb out of my career low after starring in the horror-drama The Devil's Advocate alongside Al Pacino and Charlize Theron. I took a paycut of $1 million for The Devil's Advocate so that Pacino would be cast, and later took a 90 per cent paycut for the less successful The Replacements to guarantee the casting of Gene Hackman. The Devil's Advocate did well at the box office and garnered good reviews.


  • 1999

    The 1999 science fiction-action hit The Matrix, a film in which I had a starring role, was a box office success and attracted positive reviews.


  • 2000

    Entitled to British citizenship through my English mother. I hold Canadian citizenship by naturalization. I grew up as a Canadian and identify as such, and hold an American green card. My biological father was born in the United States but I required a green card because I was not eligible to claim automatic citizenship by birth abroad to one United States citizen. In January 2000, my girlfriend, Jennifer Syme, gave birth eight months into her pregnancy to Ava Archer Syme-Reeves, who was stillborn. The strain put on their relationship by their grief resulted in my and Syme's breakup several weeks later.


  • 2001

    On April 2, 2001, Syme was driving alone on Los Angeles' Cahuenga Boulevard when she sideswiped three parked cars, rolled over several times and was thrown from the car. Authorities believed she died instantly. She reportedly was being treated for depression and taking two prescription drugs, which police found in her car. I sought "peace and time to deal with this", according to my friend Bret Domrose, a guitarist in Reeves's alternative rock band Dogstar.


  • 2005

    In between the first Matrix film and its sequels, I received positive reviews for my portrayal of an abusive husband in The Gift. Aside from The Gift, I appeared in several films that received mostly negative reviews and unimpressive box office grosses, including The Watcher, Sweet November, and The Replacements. However, the two Matrix sequels, The Matrix Reloaded and The Matrix Revolutions, along with Something's Gotta Give and the 2005 horror-action film, Constantine, were box office successes and brought me back into the public spotlight.


  • 2005

    In early 2005, my accomplishments in Hollywood were recognized by the Hollywood Walk of Fame with a star located at 6801 Hollywood Boulevard.


  • 2006

    My appearance in A Scanner Darkly (2006), based on the dystopian science fiction novel by Philip K. Dick, received favourable reviews, and The Lake House, my romantic outing with Sandra Bullock, was a success at the box office.


  • 2008

    I went on to play the lead character in two 2008 films, Street Kings and The Day the Earth Stood Still.


  • 2008

    Beginning in 2008, I began pre-production on his directorial debut, Man of Tai Chi. The film is a multilingual narrative, partly inspired by the life of his friend, stuntman Tiger Chen. Filming occurred on mainland China and Hong Kong.


  • 2009

    In February 2009 starred in director Rebecca Miller's film The Private Life of Pippa Lee, which premiered at Berlinale.


  • 2010

The images were soon distributed via several blogs and news sites. These pictures led to the "Keanu is Sad" or "Sad Keanu" meme being spread on internet forums.


  • 2010

    During Man of Tai Chi's five years of scripting and production, acted in several B movies with lead roles as Henry in 2010's Henry's Crime and John in 2012's Generation Um.... During that time, also played Kai in the critically panned 47 Ronin. Critics widely attribute the film's poor performance to its direction, pacing, focus on special effects, and editing.


  • 2011

    In 2011, produced the documentary Side by Side about the supplanting of photo-chemical film by digital camera technology; I interviewed several celebrated directors including James Cameron, Martin Scorsese, and Christopher Nolan.


  • 2013

    My first directorial film, Man of Tai Chi, premiered in 2013 with showings at the Beijing Film Festival and Cannes Film Festival. The work was awarded in Beijing and praised by recognized director of action genre films, John Woo. I continued acting while exploring other forms of artistry.


  • 2013

    I further elaborated on the film in December 2013 during a taping of NBC's The Today Show. "I'm open to the idea of that...I think it's pretty surreal, playing 'Bill & Ted' at 50. But we have a good story in that. You can see the life and joy in those characters, and I think the world can always use some life and joy." I has been linked to the maritime romance The Modern Ocean.


  • 2014

    In October 2014, played the title role in the action thriller John Wick. The film, which stars me as a retired hitman, opened to positive reviews and performed well at the box office.


  • 2016

    In 2016, appeared in the horror thriller The Neon Demon and the dystopian romance The Bad Batch. I had a cameo in the Jordan Peele/Keegan-Michael Key action comedy Keanu where I voiced the eponymous kitten. Keanu director Peter Atencio revealed that the filmmakers had contacted my management about me appearing in the film, who declined on his behalf. When my sister showed me the trailer, I contacted the filmmakers directly about appearing in the film. As the film had been mostly completed, they decided to have a scene where I voice the kitten.


  • 2017

    I reprised the role in John Wick: Chapter 2 (2017), which was also well received.
